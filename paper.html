Our Paper

This paper presents our methods, experiments, and findings on JPEG and JPEG-2000 for multispectral satellite imagery.

What we did in the paper

We built and analyzed two full compression pipelines for multispectral satellite images: a classic JPEG pipeline and a JPEG-2000 pipeline. For JPEG, we implemented the main steps ourselves, including color space conversion, DCT, quantization, and entropy coding. For JPEG-2000, we also wrote the pipeline from scratch based on wavelet transforms, quantization, block partitioning, and entropy coding. We also describe the dataset (Earth as Art images), the lab setup, and the metrics used to evaluate performance, such as PSNR, SSIM, compression ratio, and runtime.

Our experiments explore how different design choices affect compression performance. For JPEG, we examine chroma subsampling, quantization behavior, and coefficient sparsity. For JPEG-2000, we vary parameters such as DWT level, block size, and entropy coding strategy. We conclude by comparing the strengths and trade-offs of both methods and discussing why JPEG remains widely used despite the added flexibility of JPEG-2000.
